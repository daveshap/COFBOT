Talleyrand is concerned that Napoleon's quest for total domination of Europe will destroy its economy and threaten its stability. Napoleon views the Austrians as unreliable allies. He fears that Austria will renege on its recent treaty with France and join with Russia to attack Napoleon. Napoleon's will to conquer is unquenchable. He is focused on forcing England to its knees. A prosperous life for this person would include a more peaceful approach, with less ambition for total control.
