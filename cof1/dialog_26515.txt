This is a scene from the television show "The Simpsons." In this scene, the editor of the newspaper is berating his staff for various typos and mistakes in a story. This is a common occurrence in the newsroom. It is a stressful environment where a lot of mistakes can be made and a lot of pressure is put on individuals to be quick, accurate, and insightful. This place of work is a great example of a place that could be high in burnout. Burnout is a state of chronic stress that is characterized by exhaustion, cynicism, and a lack of motivation. It is important for those working in a high burnout environment to find their passion and be able to have some sort of control over their work. They need to be able to feel like they are making a difference.
